  • The best contribution of Archimedes in mathematics is known as the invention of compound pulleys, antiquity, and screw pump. Students of class X have to study these chapters of compound pulleys and antiquity.
  • Archimedes was a mathematician and inventor who lived in ancient Greece, in the town of Syracuse, Sicily. He is famous for his discoveries in geometry, such as the relation between the surface and volume of a sphere and its circumscribing cylinder, the measurement formula of circles and cones, and the value of pi. He also formulated a hydrostatic principle, known as Archimedes' principle, that explains the concept of buoyancy
